About Me

Hi, I'm Taylor Knapp. I'm from Charlotte, North Carolina and attended the NC School of Science and Mathematics (co'19). I graduated from Brown University in 2023 and received an Honors Sc.B. in Astrophysics and Pure Mathematics. I am now a third year graduate student in physics at Caltech studying black holes, gravitational waves, and numerical relativity. If you're interested in my published research and past presentations, please check out this tab. My full CV is linked on this tab.

Outside of physics, I'm interested in the history of science and early astronomy. I've linked some of my articles and research on Ancient Near-East Astronomy here. I'm also interested in how I can apply the techniques and approaches that I've cultivated in physics to other professional contexts. For my professional work experiences, please check out this tab. I'm also an avid runner, crafter, and reader in my free time. If you have any good book recommendations, I'm always open!

If you want to get in contact with me, my email and other sites are collected on this tab. Feel free to reach out to me and I'll do my best to respond quickly. :)

History of Science

In my third year at Brown University, I enrolled in a Mathematics in the Ancient World course in the Assyriology Department. During that course, I found a secondary passion for understanding where the tools and traditions of modern science and mathematics come from. I continued with courses in this department and ultimately became involved with research in Assyriology and Ancient Near-East Astronomy under John Steele. I have published two peer-reviewed articles and one popular science article on the topic. While I am currently engaged in a PhD program in Astrophysics, I try to stay connected to this field of study and would be interested in continuing in this field in the future. Below I have listed and linked my publications within Assyriology and Ancient Near-East Astronomy.

Knapp, T. & Steele, J. The moon wears a crown: Babylonian reports of earthshine. Astronomische Nachrichten. DOI: 10.1002/asna.20230101.

Knapp, T. The Earliest Astronomers: A Brief Overview of Babylonian Astronomy. Astrobites. (Link to Article) .

Steele, J. & Knapp, T. Calculating the Lunar Six: Evidence from Practice . Historia Mathematica. (In Prep).
